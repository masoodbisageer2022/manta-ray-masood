3D Modelling using Onshape

The entire skeletal structure of the bio-inspired manta ray was designed using Onshape, a cloud-based CAD platform that provided the flexibility and precision required for such a complex model. The initial prototype featured a finger-like extension intended to simulate the manta ray’s fins, which was fabricated using PLA filament. However, upon testing, this design proved to be impractical—it was oversized, lacked the necessary compactness, and failed to visually resemble a manta ray.

Following this, the design underwent a significant revision. A narrower body with a 3mm thickness was introduced, and specific areas were strategically cut to reduce material and increase flexibility for wing articulation. This iteration was successfully printed and performed as intended, offering the desired compactness and effective wing movement.

To enhance flexibility, a hinge mechanism was later integrated to connect the wings to the central body. Onshape’s advanced design features were instrumental in accurately drafting this joint structure.

Additionally, a gear system was developed to support mechanical movement: four small gears—each connected to a servo motor—were designed to interface with four larger gears, positioned symmetrically on both the top and bottom sections of the body. This configuration enabled efficient mechanical actuation of the wings using minimal motors, streamlining the internal mechanics. The top shell of the manta ray was also modeled to enclose and protect all electronic and mechanical components, completing the structural assembly.

Finally, a custom mold box was designed around each wing to facilitate the resin casting process. This allowed the team to apply the high-resolution skin texture—previously extracted from the 3D scanned fish model—onto the wings, giving them a realistic and tactile surface finish.
