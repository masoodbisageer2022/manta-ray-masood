3D Scanning using Handheld 3D Scanner

For the scanning phase of the project, a handheld 3D scanner provided by the university was used to capture the surface details of a real fish purchased from a local fishmonger.

(Real fish under the proper lighting ready to be scanned)

(Fish being scanned using the 3D scanner)

The specimen was carefully positioned and scanned under controlled lighting conditions to ensure accurate data capture. This process allowed for the detailed extraction of the fish’s skin texture, including the natural contours and surface irregularities. The resulting scan was processed and exported in STL format, preserving the intricate features of the fish’s skin.

The use of the handheld 3D scanner provided the result with exceptional accuracy. This level of detail was crucial for extracting realistic textures suitable for the bio-inspired design. The high-fidelity STL file could then be imported into various digital modeling and editing software platforms, where specific portions of the scanned texture could be isolated, cropped, or refined as needed. This flexibility allowed for efficient manipulation and seamless integration of the natural texture into the subsequent stages of the manta ray’s digital sculpting and design workflow.
