Finishing up with Resin (Not Implemented)

To enhance both the aesthetic realism and waterproofing of the manta ray prototype, resin coating was initially planned as the final surface treatment. Although time constraints prevented the resin application from being completed, the groundwork for the process was fully prepared. A mould structure was designed using a haptic sculpting pen, forming a box-like enclosure with a hollow interior shaped precisely like the manta ray's fin. The negative of the mould was carefully engraved with the fish scale texture previously extracted from the 3D scan, allowing the intricate details to be transferred to the resin. The idea was to pour resin into the mould, let it cure, and produce a textured, durable, and lifelike wing surface. Though unfinished, the mould design demonstrates a thoughtful approach to bio-inspired material replication and lays the foundation for future completion.
